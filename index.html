San Francisco Crime Analysis

A Comprehensive Data Story based on SF Open Data (Current & Historical)

1. Temporal Trends: When does crime happen?

Our analysis of 100,000 recent incidents shows a clear daily cycle. Crime rates are lowest in the early morning hours and peak steadily during the afternoon, specifically around 12:00 PM and 5:00 PM.

Using Small Multiples (Week 6), we can see that the temporal profile varies significantly by category. For instance, some crimes are strictly "daytime" issues, while others are more prevalent at night.

2. Geography of Crime: Hotspots & Districts

Mapping the coordinates (Latitude and Longitude) reveals that crime is highly localized. The Density Map (Heatmap) highlights intense hotspots in the city center.

When visualized by Police Districts, we can see how the different stations manage various geographical sectors of San Francisco.

3. Mathematical Insights: Power Laws & Correlations

Spatial Power Law: Our log-log plot confirms that a very small number of locations account for a vast majority of the total crime incidents, following a classic Power Law distribution.

Correlations: By comparing the weekly profiles of different crime types (like Larceny and Burglary), we found a strong correlation in their timing, suggesting they might be driven by similar urban rhythms.

4. Distribution and Variance

Finally, looking at the top 5 categories through Boxplots, we see the variance in incident times, helping us identify which crimes are most predictable in their occurrence.
